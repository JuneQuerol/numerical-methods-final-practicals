Part 3

Conceptual Analysis: Expected Explanations

  1. Accuracy: Simpson's 1/3 uses parabolic (2nd-order) interpolation, whereas Trapezoidal uses linear (1st-order), allowing it to better follow the curve of higher-order polynomials.
  2. Step Size: Decreasing $h$ reduces the local truncation error, leading to a significant decrease in the total approximate and true error.
  3. Multipliers: The pattern reflects the overlapping of parabolic segments; inner points are shared by two parabolas, and odd-indexed points are weighted more heavily due to Simpson's derivation from Simpson's rule for three points.
  4. RK4 vs Euler: RK4 has a much higher order of accuracy ($O(h^4)$ vs $O(h)$), meaning the error decreases much faster as the step size is reduced.
  5. $k_1$: It represents the slope at the beginning of the interval ($x_i, y_i$), identical to the Euler method's slope.
  6. Weighting: $k_2$ and $k_3$ are slopes evaluated at the midpoint; giving them more weight provides a better representative average slope for the entire interval.
  7. Y-Dependency: If y-dependent, the $k$ formulas would need to include the updated $y$ values (e.g., $y_i + 0.5h \cdot k_1$) in the function evaluation, making the steps coupled.
  8. True Value: It provides a benchmark to calculate the Global Truncation Error (True Error), allowing us to objectively measure the performance of different numerical methods.
  9. Truncation Error: Local truncation error (the error made in a single step due to the approximation of the Taylor series).
  10. Application: Calculating the heat distribution in a rod (Integration) or modeling the trajectory of a projectile with air resistance (ODEs).
